Severity Zone & Remaining LifeISO Severity Zone
A standard scale (A→D) that tells you how serious the vibration level is. Zone A = healthy. Zone B = acceptable but watch it. Zone C = action needed soon. Zone D = danger, stop the machine.

RUL = Remaining Useful Life — an estimate of how many days before the machine needs maintenance.ISO 10816-3:2009 · ISO 13381-1:2015

Fault Severity RadarFault Severity Radar
Shows how strongly the vibration signature matches each known fault type. The bigger the spike toward a fault name, the stronger the evidence for that fault in your data. Red/orange spikes = top suspects.ISO 13379-1:2012 Annex A

FFT Frequency SpectrumFFT — Fast Fourier Transform
This chart breaks your vibration signal into its individual frequency components — like separating a chord into individual notes. Each spike is a specific vibration frequency. The pattern of spikes tells us what type of fault is present.

Red spikes = the strongest fault frequency and its harmonics (repeating multiples).ISO 13373-2:2016 §7.2

Fault ClassificationFault Classification
Each bar shows how likely a specific fault type is, based on the frequency pattern and vibration statistics from your data. Scored 0–100%. Above 60% = strong evidence. Below 40% = weak / indicative only.ISO 13379-1:2012 §5

What do these fault types mean?Common Fault Types
• BPFO — Outer race bearing defect (most common)
• BPFI — Inner race bearing defect
• BSF — Rolling ball/element defect
• FTF — Bearing cage defect
• Imbalance — Unequal weight distribution on rotor
• Misalignment — Shaft not aligned correctly
• Looseness — Loose bolts, bearings or mountings
• Electrical — Motor winding or rotor bar faultsISO 13379-1:2012 Annex A
